BIOGRAPHY

Nathan Halbur is a baritone* and composer/arranger based in Boston, Massachusetts.

Growing up in California, my musical endeavors were originally focused on concert percussion and jazz/pop piano. I studied Physics as an undergraduate at the University of California, Davis. While there I received voice instruction from Zoila Muñoz and Dr. Robin Fisher, appeared as Figaro in Mozart’s Le nozze di Figaro, and was Musical Director of the Davis Chamber Choir (which entailed conducting, composing, arranging, and singing).

Since then I have worked professionally as a singer in both solo and choral capacities, moving from California to Boston in 2019. As a soloist, I have performed primarily concert works on both coasts. A recent highlight was Mendelssohn’s Die erste Walpurgisnacht in NEC’s Jordan Hall with Chorus Pro Musica and the Metropolitan Chorale, described in The Boston Musical Intelligencer thusly:

…the dramatic highpoint came in the call and answer discourse between a Druid watchman (Nathan Halbur, a Metropolitan Chorale Artist in Residence, in a well-projected baritone) with his uproarious pagan compatriots. “These insufferable Papist Christians/ let’s cleverly outwit them!…Come, with prongs and pitchforks like the very devils they invented… Join us in our whirling howl!” Who could resist?

I have also created opportunities of my own through Causeway Chamber Soloists. One recent endeavor was a recital of French mélodie featuring songs by Fauré, Debussy, Ravel, and Poulenc.

As a choral singer, I am experienced in secular and liturgical contexts. I’ve performed section-leader duties, as with the Metropolitan Chorale and at St. Cecilia Parish (where I sing weekly); and I’ve been part of fully professional ensembles, as at Church of the Advent and Emmanuel Music (with which I sang Bach’s B-minor mass in 2019). In summer of 2019 I participated in the Seraphic Fire Professional Choral Institute at the Aspen Music Festival, where I appeared in concert with Seraphic Fire and was the bass soloist in a masterclass performance of Handel’s Jephtha under the direction of Scott Jarrett.

I am well-versed in music production, and have the audio equipment and skills to make professional-quality recordings from home, for virtual-choir purposes or otherwise.


*While I am most accurately described as a baritone, I am comfortable in much of the bass and bass-baritone repertoire. In choirs, I sing both Bass 2 and Bass 1 as needed.
